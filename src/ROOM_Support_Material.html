VILLAGE at Junction Festival, 2016

Brienna Macnish: creative concept, lead artist, creative producer, Clare McCracken: key collaborator, visual design, Robert D Jordan: key collaborator, composition, sound design and sound system design

VILLAGE was an immersive audio documentary and installation work created over twelve months with the residents of retirement communities in Launceston, Tasmania. Through an audio-documentary that shares the stories older people, VILLAGE is a gesture towards how intergenerational connection within our communities might begin. In conjunction the audio/installation work, two participatory satellite events were run as part of VILLAGE. "Let's Talk About Death", was an advanced care directive workshop produced with Palliative Care Tasmania and "Common Ground" was a intergenerational afternoon tea connecting older and younger female leaders.

VILAGE was commissioned by Junction Festival and supported by Creative Victoria, the Australia Council, significant in-kind support was provided by Word of Mouth Technology, All About Gardens, Laucneston and BMW Launceston. The project engaged with over twenty older people from five retirement communities in Launceston.

This documentation demonstrates the artist's capacity for working with communities of older people, ability to build support for their work within the aged services sector and demonstrated sucess bringing non-arts industry partners (such as our audio technology provider, Word of Mouth Technology) onboard projects.

HOME at Next Wave Festival, 2014

Brienna Macnish: creative concept, Robert D Jordan: composition, sound design and sound system design

HOME was a site-specific audio work that was experienced by a single audience member in the home of an older person. The work spoke about ageing in contemporary Australia, in particular, about being the last one left in a family home when your children are grown and partner is gone.

This project was commissioned by Next Wave Festival and supported by Creative Victoria, Australia Council, City of Melboure and Hothouse Theatre.

This project was created in close collaboration with Marian Neal, a 70 year old woman. During this project we built relationships with organisations such as seniors groups and University of the Third Age. By undertaking this project we have demonstrated our competance working with older people and community organisations.

Section 32 at Immerse Festival, 2016

Clare McCracken: lead artist, creative producer, Brienna Macnish: key collaborator, performance director, Robert D Jordan: key collaborator, composition, sound design and sound system design, Jessie Stanley: associate artist

Section 32 was an immersive installation that transformed the inside and outside of a suburban home into a vision of the future.

Exploring key themes within futurist literature such as climate change, rapid population growth, housing affordability, technological advances such as driverless cars and shifts in the labour market Section 32 looks at the way our domestic lives may change in the specific context of the Australian suburbs. Over four weeks, artists transformed a suburban house inside and out creating an immersive installation with performance that transported audiences to the end of this century to experience the future of the Australian suburbs.

Section 32 was supported by Knox City Council and an anonymous donor.

This project demonstrates that Brienna, Clare and Robert have the capacity to create large scale, high quality cross-artform installation work.

The Becalmed Heart

Brienna Macnish: lead artist, creative producer, Clare McCracken: key collaborator, visual design, Robert D Jordan: key collabroator, sound design and composition, Christopher Page: lighting design

The Becalmed Heart was a large scale installation work that was presented at the 2015 Underbelly Arts Festival by the same collaborative team who will work on VILLAGE. The project was a large-scale immersive installation that transported audiences into a world created entirely from over 20,000 plastic grocery bags.

The Becalmed Heart was built with the assistance over over 40 volenteers and was supported by The Australia Council for the Arts, Creative Victoria and two corporate partners, Replas and the Red Group from within the recycling and waste management sector.

This project demonstrates that Brienna, Clare and Robert have the capacity to create large scale, high quality cross-artform installation work.
